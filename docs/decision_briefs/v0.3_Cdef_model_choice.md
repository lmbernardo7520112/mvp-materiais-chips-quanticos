# Decision Brief — v0.3 C_def Model Choice

> **Date:** 2026-05-07
> **Status:** APPROVED by Research Council (8/8)
> **ADR:** ADR-006 (Proposed)

---

## 1. What physical problem will v0.3 model?

The spatial distribution of **thermally-generated material heterogeneities**
in a 2D domain, governed by reaction-diffusion dynamics coupled to an
existing thermal field.

Physically motivated by: point defect dynamics during silicon crystal
cooling (Dornberger 2001, Brown 1994, Voronkov 1999), where vacancies
and interstitials are generated, diffuse, and recombine as the crystal
cools through critical temperature windows.

**Reduced to:** a single effective variable C_def with generation G(T),
diffusion D(T), and recombination R(T)·C_def.

---

## 2. What does C_def represent?

C_def(x,y,t) is a **scalar, adimensional, bounded [0,1] state variable**
representing normalized density of thermally-generated heterogeneities.

It can be interpreted (not equated) as analogous to:
- Normalized vacancy concentration (crystal growth analogy)
- Trap-like charge disorder density (device analogy)
- Generic defect-like material heterogeneity

---

## 3. What does C_def NOT represent?

- ❌ Real vacancy concentration in silicon (cm⁻³)
- ❌ Real interstitial concentration
- ❌ Real interface trap density (Pb centers, Dit)
- ❌ Calibrated charge noise source
- ❌ Any quantity validated against experiment
- ❌ Anything from Maurand et al.

---

## 4. Why is Maurand et al. a good starting point but not the model?

**Good because:**
- Demonstrates that silicon CMOS is a strategic platform for spin qubits
- Shows that charge noise, traps, and process variability limit qubit
  performance
- Motivates the entire process-to-device bridge

**Not the model because:**
- Does not contain reaction-diffusion equations
- Does not provide kinetic parameters (D₀, E_D, A_G, E_R, ...)
- Does not model defect dynamics during thermal processing
- Is a device demonstration paper, not a modeling paper

**Correct citation:** device-target anchor. Never calibration source.

---

## 5. Why not the full V/I model now?

The full vacancy/interstitial coupled system (Dornberger 2001):

```
∂C_V/∂t = ∇·(D_V∇C_V) − k_rec(C_V·C_I − C_V^eq·C_I^eq)
∂C_I/∂t = ∇·(D_I∇C_I) − k_rec(C_V·C_I − C_I^eq·C_V^eq)
```

Requires:
- 10+ parameters per species (D₀_V, E_m^V, E_f^V, D₀_I, E_m^I, E_f^I,
  k_rec, A_V^eq, A_I^eq, ...)
- Equilibrium concentration models
- Coupled nonlinear PDEs
- Careful handling of recombination kinetics

This is **too complex** for v0.3 because:
- Parameters are poorly constrained (spreads of 2–3 orders of magnitude
  in the literature — see Crystals 11(5), 460, 2021)
- Overclaim risk: presenting uncalibrated V/I as silicon simulation
- Testing complexity increases nonlinearly with coupled equations
- The project has not yet demonstrated single-variable R-D capability

**Verdict:** defer V/I to v0.5+ when calibration data is available.

---

## 6. Why not go directly to Poisson?

Poisson requires:
```
∇²φ(x,y) = −ρ_eff(x,y) / ε
```

Where ρ_eff = q_eff · C_def. Without C_def, there is no source term.
Implementing Poisson without a process-generated defect distribution
would require assuming ρ_eff ad hoc — defeating the purpose of the
process-to-device bridge.

**v0.3 must come first** to provide C_def(x,y) as input for v0.4.

---

## 7. How does C_def prepare ρ_eff for v0.4?

The interface is clean:

```
v0.3 output:   C_def(x,y, t_final)    — adimensional field [0,1]
v0.4 input:    ρ_eff(x,y) = q_eff · C_def(x,y, t_final)
v0.4 equation: ∇²φ = −ρ_eff / ε
```

Requirements for v0.3:
- Export C_def_final as NumPy array
- Generate C_def contour figure
- Compute freeze-in metrics
- Document the C_def → ρ_eff interface in ADR-006

---

## 8. What still cannot be inferred after v0.3?

- Electrostatic potential V(x,y) — requires Poisson (v0.4)
- Confinement levels — requires Schrödinger (v0.5)
- Qubit coherence — requires quantum mechanics (out of scope)
- Gate fidelity — requires device-level modeling (out of scope)
- Real defect density — requires calibration (v0.5+)
- Comparison with TCAD — requires calibrated inputs
- Experimental validation — requires lab data

---

## 9. Minimum, simple, highly effective strategy

| Component | Choice | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Variable | Single C_def | Simplest; captures qualitative behavior |
| Equation | Standard R-D | Well-established in crystal growth literature |
| D(T) | Arrhenius | Already demonstrated in 1D solver |
| G(T) | Gaussian window | Captures thermal generation band |
| R(T,C) | Linear · Arrhenius | Simple, bounded, well-behaved |
| C_sat | 1.0 (adimensional) | Normalized, bounded [0,1] |
| BCs | Neumann no-flux | No external defect source |
| Coupling | One-way T→C | No back-coupling |
| Solver | Explicit Euler + CFL | Consistent with v0.2 approach |

This strategy is:
- **Minimal:** single variable, single equation, 6–8 parameters
- **Simple:** standard PDE, explicit solver, proven numerics
- **Effective:** produces spatial heterogeneity from thermal history
- **Defensible:** grounded in reaction-diffusion literature
- **Incremental:** builds on v0.2 infrastructure without disruption
