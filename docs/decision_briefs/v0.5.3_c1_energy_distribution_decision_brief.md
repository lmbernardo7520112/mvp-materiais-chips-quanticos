# v0.5.3 C1 Energy-Distribution Decision Brief

## Core Question

What is the smallest honest physics evolution to overcome the constant-D_it
hypothesis without advancing to C2?

## Background

C1 currently computes:

```
N_it = D_it_SI × ΔE_window
```

This assumes D_it(E) = constant within the energy window. While dimensionally
correct and useful for bookkeeping, it does not capture energy-dependent
trap distributions observed in real Si/SiO₂ interfaces.

## Comparison Table

| Profile | Formula | Phys. richness | Impl. risk | Overclaim risk | Recommendation |
|---------|---------|----------------|------------|----------------|----------------|
| P0 constant | D₀ | Low | Low | Low | Baseline (keep) |
| P1 piecewise | D_i per interval | Medium | Low–Med | Low | **Recommended** |
| P2 gaussian | A exp[−(E−E₀)²/2σ²] | Med–High | Medium | Med–High | Future |
| P3 triangular | Linear, finite support | Medium | Medium | Medium | Demo candidate |

## Dimensional Audit

For all profiles, the integral must satisfy:

```
∫ D_it(E) dE → [J⁻¹·m⁻²] × [J] = [m⁻²]
```

This is verified for all four profiles. The downstream chain:

```
σ_eff = s_charge × q_e × N_it × f_occ → [C/m²]
```

remains unchanged regardless of which profile generates N_it.

## Decision

**P1 piecewise-constant profile** is the recommended future implementation path.

**Justification:**

1. Approximates arbitrary D_it(E) without imposing artificial smoothness.
2. Preserves full transparency: each energy bin has an explicit density.
3. Easy to test: N_it = Σ D_i × ΔE_i (sum of rectangles).
4. Clear backward compatibility: single bin ≡ P0.
5. Low overclaim risk: no functional form is assumed.
6. Honest: does not pretend to know the true D_it(E) shape.

**What P1 does NOT do:**

- Does not calibrate the model.
- Does not validate device behavior.
- Does not couple to the Poisson solver.
- Does not compute ρ_eff or t_eff.
