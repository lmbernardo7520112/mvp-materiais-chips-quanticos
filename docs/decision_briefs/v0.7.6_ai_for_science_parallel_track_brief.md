# v0.7.6 AI-for-Science Parallel Track Decision Brief

## Core Question

Should AI-for-Science / PINNs / surrogate models be explored now?

## Answer

Yes, but only as a parallel, documentation-only feasibility track.

## Why Not Implement Now?

- No accepted ADR for AI-for-Science implementation.
- No dependency decision brief for ML frameworks.
- No benchmark baseline with analytic solution.
- No solver coupling baseline yet (C3 not implemented).
- No physical phi resolved by classical solver.
- No experimental device data available.
- No uncertainty quantification model defined.
- Risk of overclaim from treating ML demo as validation.

## Option Scoring

| Option | Description | Scientific value | Contamination risk | Implementation readiness | Governance fit | Score |
|--------|-------------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| AIFS-A | Documentation-only parallel feasibility | 8.0 | 1.5 | 9.0 | 9.5 | 91 |
| AIFS-B | Analytic benchmark planning | 8.5 | 2.0 | 8.0 | 9.0 | 88 |
| AIFS-C | Isolated toy PINN prototype now | 7.0 | 6.5 | 4.0 | 5.0 | 58 |
| AIFS-D | Add PyTorch/JAX dependency now | 6.0 | 8.0 | 3.5 | 3.0 | 40 |
| AIFS-E | Replace classical solver with surrogate | 8.0 (theoretical) | 9.5 | 1.0 | 1.0 | 25 |

*Score = weighted combination favoring governance fit and low contamination risk.*

## Recommendation

**Proceed with AIFS-A now.** Documentation-only parallel feasibility is the safest and most productive path.

**Prepare AIFS-B as future documentation.** Analytic benchmark specifications can be written without code or dependencies.

**Block AIFS-C/D/E** until all of the following exist: accepted ADR, dependency decision, analytic benchmarks, RED tests, isolation gates, reproducibility plan, and human approval.
