# v0.9.0 Scientific Audit — One-way Poisson Adapter

## Audit Question

Does `one_way_poisson_coupling.py` remain scientifically honest as a metadata-only adapter?

## Findings

1. **Module scope:** Strict metadata adapter, mapping charge parameters.
2. **Public API:** `build_poisson_input_source` returns dataclass structures, no math operations.
3. **Safety validators:** `validate_no_solver_runtime_coupling` and `validate_no_physical_phi_claim` are strictly enforced.
4. **Metadata invariants:** Assertions ensure `physical_phi_allowed=False` and `solver_coupling_enabled=False`.
5. **Boundary metadata:** Documented strictly as placeholder dimensions, no internal geometry built.
6. **Charge-accounting semantics:** Maps raw electron numbers, stopping before physical density calculation.
7. **Absence of solver runtime:** `solve_poisson` or similar routines do not exist.
8. **Absence of physical phi:** Output represents source metadata, not potential fields.
9. **Absence of calibration claims:** No claims of real-world device properties.
10. **Absence of device prediction:** Completely isolated from quantum coherent predictions.

## Scientific Claim Boundary

### Allowed claims
* metadata-only adapter;
* demonstrative contract;
* non-runtime one-way source representation;
* no physical interpretation;
* no calibration;
* no device prediction.

### Not allowed claims
* physical electrostatic potential;
* experimentally validated phi;
* calibrated device response;
* quantum device prediction;
* coherence prediction;
* production solver coupling.

## Verdict

* SCIENTIFICALLY HONEST AS METADATA-ONLY
