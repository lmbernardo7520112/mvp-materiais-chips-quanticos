## Expert 1 — Research Software Engineer

**Recommendation:** C1.

C1 is testable without the solver. The 8 future RED tests listed in ADR-009
are pure-arithmetic checks — no PDE, no convergence, no stiffness. This
means TDD RED→GREEN can proceed quickly and confidently. C2 would require
solver-level integration tests that are harder to isolate.

---

## Expert 2 — Semiconductor Device Physicist

**Recommendation:** C1.

The D_it → N_it → σ_eff chain is standard in MOS physics (Sze & Ng, ch. 4;
Nicollian & Brews). C1 implements the bookkeeping correctly: D_it is an
energy-dependent quantity, N_it is the integrated areal density, and σ_eff
is the surface charge. The sign convention (donor-like vs acceptor-like)
must remain an explicit parameter, not hardcoded.

**Caveat:** δE_window should not be silently defaulted to kT or E_g. In a
real device, the relevant window depends on the Fermi level position, which
C1 does not compute. Treating δE_window as an explicit input is correct.

---

## Expert 3 — Computational Materials Scientist

**Recommendation:** C1.

The key insight is that C1 avoids the σ → ρ conversion (dividing by t_eff).
This is where most dimensional confusion occurs in simplified models. By
stopping at σ_eff [C/m²], C1 produces a physically meaningful quantity that
can be validated independently of the solver geometry.

**Concern:** C1 results cannot be directly compared with the current Poisson
output because they live in different dimensional spaces (C/m² vs the solver's
dimensionless ρ_eff). This limits immediate scientific utility but preserves
correctness.

---

## Expert 4 — Applied Mathematician / PDE Specialist

**Recommendation:** C1 with a caveat.

C1 is mathematically clean because it avoids the PDE entirely. However, I
note that σ_eff is not directly useful until it becomes a source term in
a PDE. The scientific value of C1 is in **preparation**, not in producing
new simulation results.

**Requirement for C2:** Before coupling σ_eff to the solver (via ρ_eff =
σ_eff / t_eff), a manufactured solution test with physical ε must pass.
This is non-negotiable — the solver's behavior with ε ≈ 10⁻¹⁰ has not
been characterized.

---

## Expert 5 — Numerical Methods Specialist

**Recommendation:** C1.

I agree with Expert 4. C1 is orthogonal to the solver, which means no
risk of numerical instability. When C2 is eventually proposed, the solver
will need: (a) relative tolerance instead of absolute, (b) condition
number monitoring, and (c) a physical-ε manufactured solution. None of
these exist yet.

## Expert 6 — Skeptical External Reviewer

**Recommendation:** C1, but with **objections**.

### Objection 1: Is C1 just more bookkeeping?

C1 computes σ_eff but never uses it in a simulation. The output is a
number with units [C/m²] that sits alongside a demonstrative φ with no
physical meaning. How is this different from more metadata?

**Response (Expert 2):** C1 is dimensionally grounded, not just a label.
The D_it conversion factor (6.242e22) is physics, not metadata. The tests
verify dimensional correctness, not just string labels. C1 produces a
testable physical intermediate, even if it's not yet coupled to the solver.

### Objection 2: When does the model actually produce new physics?

The project is at v0.4.9 and still has not produced a single physically
interpretable φ. At what point does governance stop and science start?

**Response (Expert 1):** C1 is the inflection point. After C1 is
implemented and tested, C2 can couple charge to the solver with a known-good
upstream chain. The methodology audit correctly identified that governance
matured faster than physics — C1 is the course correction.

### Objection 3: Could we skip C1 and go straight to C2?

If the goal is a physically interpretable simulation, C2 gets there
faster. C1 feels like an intermediate that delays the result.

**Response (Expert 4):** C2 without validated σ_eff means coupling an
untested dimensional chain to the solver. If the D_it conversion has an
error, the solver will produce wrong φ and we won't know whether the bug
is in the source term or the solver. C1 isolates the source chain for
independent verification.

**Response (Expert 6, accepting):** I accept the staged approach. C1 is
justified as an isolation strategy, not just caution.
