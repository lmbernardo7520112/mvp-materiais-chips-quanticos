# v0.8.6 ADR-016 Acceptance Council

## Council Members & Votes

### 1. Computational Physicist — Poisson/electrostatics
* **Vote:** Accept
* **Justification:** The one-way coupling establishes the correct mathematical boundary value problem without introducing unstable self-consistency before we are ready.
* **Principal Risk:** Numerical instability at material interfaces if grid resolution is too coarse.
* **Mandatory Gate Before Future RED:** Grid convergence constraints must be enforced.

### 2. Semiconductor Device Physicist
* **Vote:** Accept
* **Justification:** As long as we don't claim physical validation, passing charge to a solver is a standard next step to test the pipeline.
* **Principal Risk:** Outputs being misinterpreted as actual band bending.
* **Mandatory Gate Before Future RED:** `not_for_physical_interpretation` metadata tags on all outputs.

### 3. Numerical Methods Specialist
* **Vote:** Accept
* **Justification:** Decoupling the electrostatics from the charge-filling loop avoids non-linear oscillations during early architectural testing.
* **Principal Risk:** Divergence of the linear solver if charge density is unphysically large.
* **Mandatory Gate Before Future RED:** Sanity checks on maximum charge density before passing to Poisson.

### 4. Scientific Software Architect
* **Vote:** Accept
* **Justification:** This perfectly aligns with our incremental demonstrative strategy (Option B). It validates the data flow.
* **Principal Risk:** Breaking C1/C2/C3 isolation.
* **Mandatory Gate Before Future RED:** Ensure the solver only reads the final C3 projected grid.

### 5. AI-RSE Governance Lead
* **Vote:** Accept
* **Justification:** The guardrails are extremely robust. BudgetOps, Usage Ledger, and the Non-Authorization Clause provide sufficient protection.
* **Principal Risk:** A future agent trying to implement this autonomously without human supervision.
* **Mandatory Gate Before Future RED:** Human approval verified on the RED plan.

### 6. Research Integrity Auditor
* **Vote:** Accept
* **Justification:** The explicit bans on calibration claims and device prediction prevent scientific overclaiming.
* **Principal Risk:** Accidental leakage of physical terms in documentation or variable names.
* **Mandatory Gate Before Future RED:** Strict code review against "physical phi" terminology.

### 7. Skeptical External Reviewer
* **Vote:** Accept
* **Justification:** The strict isolation from real physics makes this acceptable as a pure software engineering exercise.
* **Principal Risk:** Slippery slope towards real physics without proper scientific review.
* **Mandatory Gate Before Future RED:** All 6 objections addressed formally.

### 8. Cost/BudgetOps Auditor
* **Vote:** Accept
* **Justification:** Budget limits are verified. The future implementation should be well within standard CI limits.
* **Principal Risk:** Excessive LLM loops during the future RED phase.
* **Mandatory Gate Before Future RED:** `max_autonomous_goal_loops` capped to 0 for execution, strictly assist-only.

### 9. Reproducibility Engineer
* **Vote:** Accept
* **Justification:** The pipeline remains deterministic and bounded.
* **Principal Risk:** Non-deterministic solver behavior.
* **Mandatory Gate Before Future RED:** Fixed random seeds and strict tolerance definitions in the solver.

## Skeptical External Reviewer Objections & Responses

**Objection 1:** One-way coupling may create false physical meaning.
* **Response:** Addressed. The Non-Authorization Clause and risk matrix (R3) explicitly state the coupling is demonstrative, not physically validated.

**Objection 2:** Numerical source may be mistaken as calibrated charge.
* **Response:** Addressed. R6 enforces `calibration_status: not_calibrated` and bans calibration language.

**Objection 3:** Future Poisson output may be misread as physical phi.
* **Response:** Addressed. Outputs will be flagged as `demonstrative` and `not_for_physical_interpretation` (R2).

**Objection 4:** Boundary conditions may imply device realism.
* **Response:** Addressed. R4 mandates future RED tests must verify BCs are demonstrative and do not encode specific device geometry.

**Objection 5:** `generate_all_results` may create physical-looking artifacts.
* **Response:** Addressed. R10 mandates demonstrative flags in all CSV headers and figure titles.

**Objection 6:** BudgetOps/Usage Ledger may be bypassed in future implementation.
* **Response:** Addressed. R11 and R12 strictly require BudgetOps and Usage Ledger validation in all future implementation steps.
