## Questions Addressed

### Q1: Is the process-to-device direction pertinent?

**Consensus: YES.** The chain from process defects to electrostatic
environment is a real and documented phenomenon. Interface traps generated
during CMOS processing do affect the potential landscape seen by confined
charges. Studying this chain computationally is a legitimate research
direction.

### Q2: Is Maurand et al. 2016 an adequate anchor?

**Consensus: YES, as device-target anchor only.** Maurand demonstrates
a CMOS spin qubit in silicon, establishing the relevance of CMOS process
quality for qubit performance. However, the paper does not provide
process-level parameters, D_it profiles, or simulation methodology that
this project could directly use.

**Specialist 3 (Qubit Physicist):** "Maurand is the right motivating
example, but the project must not claim to reproduce or predict the
performance of that specific device."

### Q3: Is the project ready for Schrödinger?

**Consensus: NO.**

**Specialist 4 (Quantum Simulation):** "Implementing Schrödinger without
a physically motivated confining potential V(x) is meaningless. You need
ρ_eff → Poisson → φ first. Otherwise you're solving Schrödinger in
an arbitrary potential with no connection to the defect chain."

**Specialist 6 (Mathematician):** "The eigenvalue problem is numerically
straightforward. The hard part is getting the right V(x). Don't jump
to the easy part while skipping the hard part."

### Q4: Is the project ready for C2 (ρ_eff)?

**Consensus: NOT YET, but it's the correct next physics layer.**

**Specialist 1 (Device Physicist):** "The mapping σ_eff → ρ_eff requires
defining t_eff — either a physical oxide thickness or a numerical smearing
parameter. This must be decided explicitly, documented in an ADR, and
justified. The choice affects all downstream physics."

**Specialist 5 (Metrology):** "Make sure you distinguish between
ρ_eff as a numerical construct (charge spread over a volume for Poisson)
and ρ_eff as a physical quantity. At this stage, it should be treated as
a numerical device."

### Q5: What is the correct next layer?

**Consensus: Curated E1/E2 profile library (v0.6.2), then C2 ADR (v0.7.0).**

The profile library strengthens the input data for all downstream layers
without introducing new physics coupling.

### Q6: What must remain blocked?

**Consensus:**
- Coherence prediction (T₁, T₂)
- Gate fidelity
- Calibration claims
- TCAD/QTCAD replacement claims
- C2 before ADR
- Schrödinger before Poisson coupling

### Q7: What data would be needed for calibration claims?

**Specialist 2 (TCAD Engineer):** "Calibration would require
experimentally measured D_it(E) profiles from specific process runs,
ideally from charge pumping or conductance methods, plus device-level
C-V or I-V data for validation. This is completely outside the scope
of an academic MVP."

**Specialist 8 (Skeptic):** "Even with such data, calibration claims
would require uncertainty quantification, sensitivity analysis, and
independent reproduction. I see no path to calibration within the
current project scope."

### Q8: Which roadmap is safest?

**Consensus: Option C — layer-by-layer.**

## Skeptical Objections

### Objection 1: "The project may never reach device layer"

**Raised by:** Specialist 8 (Skeptic)

"31 releases and only now reaching D_it(E) integration. At this rate,
Schrödinger is years away. The governance overhead may be killing
physics progress."

**Response:** The governance overhead is proportional to the overclaim
risk. Each layer is small but auditable. The alternative (jumping
ahead) risks producing physically meaningless results.

### Objection 2: "Maurand anchor is aspirational, not operational"

**Raised by:** Specialist 8 (Skeptic)

"The project cannot reproduce anything from Maurand et al. The anchor
is motivational, not functional. Be explicit about this."

**Response:** Agreed. ADR-011 explicitly states Maurand is a
device-target anchor only, not a calibration or parameter source.

### Objection 3: "Poisson-Schrödinger is standard — just use FEniCS or QTCAD"

**Raised by:** Specialist 2 (TCAD Engineer)

"Why reinvent the wheel? Poisson-Schrödinger solvers exist."

**Response:** The project's value is not in the solver itself but in
the auditable chain from process defects to confinement. Using external
solvers may be considered in future, but the MVP must demonstrate the
chain with full traceability.

### Objection 4: "t_eff is the elephant in the room"

**Raised by:** Specialist 1 (Device Physicist)

"The mapping σ_eff → ρ_eff depends entirely on how you define t_eff.
This is not a trivial decision and has major implications for
the physical meaning of φ."

**Response:** Agreed. This is exactly why C2 requires its own ADR
with explicit definition of t_eff semantics.
