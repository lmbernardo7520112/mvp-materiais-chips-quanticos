## Deliberation

### 1. Semiconductor Device Physicist

> ADR-010 correctly identifies the limitation of constant D_it and proposes
> a physically motivated evolution. The piecewise-constant profile (P1) is
> the minimal honest step forward. The evidence taxonomy (E0–E4) ensures we
> know exactly what level of physics claim each profile carries.
>
> **Can ADR-010 be accepted?** Yes.
> **P1 correct next step?** Yes.
> **Option B prudent?** Yes — literature-informed bins are well-grounded.
> **Option C acceptable?** Yes, with strict E2 metadata requirements.
> **E3/E4 blocked?** Must remain blocked.
> **C1 scope preserved?** Yes.
> **Vote:** Accept ADR-010.

### 2. Interface Trap Metrology Specialist

> The v0.5.4 evidence review covers the relevant measurement techniques.
> The C-V conductance method is correctly identified as the gold standard
> for D_it(E) profiling. The minimum source quality criteria are adequate.
>
> My concern: when implementing P1, the bin edges must be clearly defined
> relative to a stated energy reference (E_v, E_c, or midgap). The
> v0.5.3 future TDD plan includes energy-reference tests, which addresses
> this.
>
> **Can ADR-010 be accepted?** Yes.
> **Option B prudent?** Yes.
> **Option C acceptable?** Only if energy reference is documented.
> **Risks?** R3 (energy reference mismatch) must be tested in RED phase.
> **Vote:** Accept ADR-010.

### 3. Silicon Spin Qubit / Charge Noise Specialist

> For qubit applications, the relevant D_it is near the Fermi level in the
> dot (~0.1–0.3 eV below E_c). A piecewise profile allows assigning
> different densities to this region vs. midgap vs. band edge. This is a
> meaningful improvement over constant D_it for noise modeling context.
>
> ADR-010 is mature. The two councils and feasibility review provide
> sufficient scientific due diligence.
>
> **Can ADR-010 be accepted?** Yes.
> **Vote:** Accept ADR-010.

### 4. Computational Materials Scientist

> P1 piecewise is numerically clean and maps directly to quadrature. The
> backward compatibility (single bin = P0) is elegant. DFT-informed
> transition levels could populate bins in the future, but this requires
> E2 metadata — correctly gated by the evidence taxonomy.
>
> **Can ADR-010 be accepted?** Yes.
> **Vote:** Accept ADR-010.

### 5. Applied Mathematician

> The integral formulation N_it = Σ D_i × ΔE_i is a Riemann sum — the
> simplest and most transparent numerical integration scheme. Error
> analysis is straightforward: O(h × max|D_it'|) per bin. The future TDD
> plan includes convergence and monotonicity tests.
>
> The evidence taxonomy correctly separates the numerical method (P1) from
> the physics claim (evidence level). This is good mathematical hygiene.
>
> **Can ADR-010 be accepted?** Yes.
> **Vote:** Accept ADR-010.

### 6. Research Software Engineer

> From a governance perspective, ADR-010 has been through:
> - v0.5.3: proposal, 6-specialist council, TDD plan, risk matrix
> - v0.5.4: evidence taxonomy, 7-specialist council, feasibility review
> - v0.5.5: acceptance review with 15 criteria (all PASS)
>
> This is more due diligence than most ADRs receive. The RED-before-code
> constraint is clear. The acceptance gates are defined.
>
> **Can ADR-010 be accepted?** Yes.
> **Risks?** Only that acceptance might be interpreted as authorization to
> skip RED tests. Mitigation: acceptance gates G6 and G15 require RED.
> **Vote:** Accept ADR-010.

### 7. Skeptical External Reviewer

> **Objection 1:** Accepting ADR-010 creates pressure to implement P1
> quickly. The team might rush through RED tests to get to GREEN, producing
> superficial coverage. The 10 future RED tests listed in v0.5.3 are
> ambitious — can they all be meaningfully implemented?
>
> **Response:** Valid concern. Mitigation: the TDD skill enforces strict
> RED-GREEN-REFACTOR separation. Each test must fail before implementation.
> The acceptance gates require RED tests before any code. Implementation
> will be a dedicated v0.6.0 release, not rushed into v0.5.5.
>
> **Objection 2:** The evidence taxonomy allows E2 profiles (digitized
> experimental curves). Once ADR-010 is accepted, someone might input an E2
> profile and present σ_eff as "experimentally informed," blurring the
> line between C1 bookkeeping and C2 device modeling.
>
> **Response:** Valid and important. Mitigation: the acceptance note
> explicitly states that E2 profiles do not authorize calibration, device
> prediction, ρ_eff, t_eff, or solver coupling. The risk matrix (R7, R10)
> addresses this. All CSV outputs must declare calibration_status =
> not_calibrated.
>
> **Objection 3:** Two councils and three documentation releases (v0.5.3,
> v0.5.4, v0.5.5) for a scope decision that authorizes zero code seems
> like governance overhead. Is this proportionate?
>
> **Response:** For a physics-first MVP where overclaim risk is the primary
> threat, this level of due diligence is proportionate. The alternative —
> accepting an under-reviewed ADR — would undermine the governance model
> that has kept the project clean through 34 PRs.
>
> **Vote:** Accept ADR-010, with the explicit condition that the acceptance
> note includes all blocking constraints.

## Council Decision

1. **ADR-010 is accepted** as the governing scope for future C1
   energy-distribution modeling.
2. **P1 piecewise D_it(E)** is the authorized next implementation step.
3. **Option B** (literature-informed) is the default profile strategy.
4. **Option C** (E2 experimental-profile prior) is allowed only with
   complete source metadata.
5. **E3/E4** require a dedicated calibration ADR — not authorized now.
6. **Implementation** requires a future RED phase (v0.6.0+) with all
   10 defined tests.
7. **C2 remains blocked.** No ρ_eff, t_eff, solver coupling, or φ.
8. **Skeptical objections** are incorporated into the acceptance note.
