## Deliberation

### 1. Semiconductor Device Physicist

> The U-shaped D_it(E) distribution at Si/SiO₂ is well-established. Using
> literature shapes to inform P1 bins is physically sound at E1 level. The
> key is to use the *shape* (low at midgap, high near edges) rather than
> claiming specific *values* match the target device.
>
> **Evidence level:** E1 acceptable now, E2 conditional.
> **Recommended techniques:** C-V conductance, charge pumping.
> **Vote:** Option B default.

### 2. Interface Trap Metrology Specialist

> C-V conductance is the gold standard for D_it(E) profiling across the
> bandgap. However, the extracted D_it depends on the equivalent circuit
> model and frequency range used. When digitizing a published curve for
> P1 bins, the extraction assumptions must be documented.
>
> Charge pumping gives integrated N_it near midgap — useful for total
> validation but not for bin-level resolution.
>
> DLTS probes deep levels, which are a different trap population from
> interface states. DLTS data should not be used for D_it(E) bins.
>
> **Evidence level:** E1 safe, E2 with full metadata only.
> **Required metadata:** technique, frequency range, equivalent circuit,
> temperature, energy reference.
> **Vote:** Option B default, Option C conditional.

### 3. Silicon Spin Qubit / Charge Noise Specialist

> For spin qubit applications, the relevant D_it is near the Fermi level
> in the quantum dot, which sits ~0.1–0.3 eV below the conduction band
> edge in typical gate-defined dots. The 1/f noise spectroscopy gives an
> effective density at this energy but cannot resolve the full D_it(E).
>
> Literature-informed bins (E1) using the U-shape are reasonable. The
> midgap bin is most relevant to qubit noise; the band-edge bins provide
> context but less direct relevance.
>
> **Evidence level:** E1 sufficient for current goals.
> **Vote:** Option B default.

### 4. Computational Materials Scientist

> DFT calculations can provide defect formation energies and transition
> levels, but mapping these to D_it(E) requires assumptions about defect
> concentrations. Published DFT results may inform the *position* of
> trap levels (E1) but not the *density* without further calibration.
>
> P1 piecewise with literature-informed bins is a clean numerical approach
> that separates the integration method from the physics assumptions.
>
> **Evidence level:** E1 for shapes, E2 for specific DFT-informed levels.
> **Vote:** Option B default.

### 5. Applied Mathematician

> From a numerical perspective, the choice of bin edges and densities is
> a quadrature problem. Literature-informed bins (E1) provide a structured
> quadrature rule that converges better than arbitrary uniform bins.
>
> The error analysis is straightforward: for piecewise-constant
> approximation, the error is bounded by O(h × max|D_it'|) per bin.
> Knowing the U-shape helps choose non-uniform bins that minimize error.
>
> **Vote:** Option B default.

### 6. Scientific Governance Auditor

> The evidence taxonomy (E0–E4) is essential. I verify:
> - E0/E1 are authorized and do not require additional ADR.
> - E2 requires documented metadata; does not authorize calibration.
> - E3/E4 are correctly blocked.
> - The non-calibration statement is explicit in all documents.
> - ADR-010 remains Proposed.
>
> **Vote:** Option B default, Option C conditional with E2 metadata.

### 7. Skeptical External Reviewer

> **Objection 1:** Literature D_it(E) curves from C-V conductance are
> measured on large-area MOS capacitors at room temperature. Quantum dot
> devices operate at mK temperatures with nanoscale gate geometries. The
> trap population, occupancy, and even the relevant energy range are
> fundamentally different. Transferring these curves is epistemically weak.
>
> **Response:** Valid. This is why we label transferred curves as E1
> (literature-informed shape prior), not E3 (same-stack calibration). The
> curve provides a *plausible shape* for dimensional bookkeeping, not a
> *prediction* of device behavior. The non-calibration metadata enforces
> this distinction.
>
> **Objection 2:** The U-shape is a textbook simplification. Real
> interfaces show peaks, shoulders, and orientation-dependent features.
> A 3-bin P1 approximation to a U-shape is a simplification of a
> simplification. This may give false confidence.
>
> **Response:** Valid. Mitigation: the evidence taxonomy requires noting
> the extraction assumptions and the source's limitations. The P1 profile
> is explicitly a *piecewise-constant approximation*, not a claim about
> the true D_it(E). The risk matrix (R6, R9) addresses overinterpretation.
>
> **Objection 3:** Allowing E2 profiles (digitized experimental curves)
> opens a path where someone inputs a specific D_it(E) curve and presents
> the resulting σ_eff as if the model has been "experimentally validated."
> This is scope creep toward C2.
>
> **Response:** Valid and critical. Mitigation: the acceptance gates
> (G15, G16) enforce that no E2 profile authorizes calibration or device
> prediction. The CSV metadata must declare `calibration_status =
> not_calibrated`. The feasibility brief explicitly states that E2 is a
> "prior," not a validation. Gate G16 requires evidence-level labels in
> all future outputs.
>
> **Vote:** Option B default, Option C conditional with strict metadata
> requirements. Reject any loosening of C1/C2 boundary.

## Vote Summary

| Specialist | Vote |
|------------|------|
| Semiconductor Device Physicist | B default |

**Result:** Unanimous — **Option B (literature-informed) as default.**
Option C (experimental-profile prior) allowed conditionally with full E2
metadata. E3/E4 blocked. C2 blocked.

## Council Decision

1. **Option B** is the recommended default for future P1 implementation.
2. **Option C** is allowed only when E2 metadata criteria are fully met.
3. **E3/E4** require a dedicated calibration ADR — not authorized now.
4. **ADR-010** remains Proposed; acceptance review should include
   evidence-level labeling requirement.
5. **C2 remains blocked.** No ρ_eff, t_eff, solver coupling, or φ.
6. **Skeptical objections** are incorporated into the risk matrix.
