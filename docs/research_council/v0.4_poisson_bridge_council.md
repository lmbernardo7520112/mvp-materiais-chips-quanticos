## Item 1 — Boussinesq Analogy

**Expert 1 (Materials):** The Boussinesq approximation in solidification
converts small perturbations of temperature and composition into a buoyancy
source term:

```
ρ ≈ ρ₀ [1 − βT(T − Tref) + βC(c − cref)]
```

The key insight is that the full density is never computed; only the
**perturbation** δρ drives the momentum equation. This avoids catastrophic
numerical errors from subtracting nearly equal large numbers.

**Expert 2 (Numerics):** The analogy is structurally sound. In Poisson's
equation, the source term plays the same role as buoyancy in Navier-Stokes.
Using a perturbation form δρ_eff rather than absolute ρ_eff is essential for
numerical conditioning and for defining a reference/background charge state, which supports charge-neutral solvability.

**Expert 3 (Devices):** The analogy must not be pushed to identity. Mass
density ρ and charge density ρ_eff have different dimensions, different
physics, and different coupling mechanisms. The structural parallel
(perturbation → source → field equation) is valid. The physical
identification is not.

---

## Item 2 — Mass Density vs. Charge Density

**Expert 3 (Devices):**
- Mass density ρ [kg/m³]: continuous, positive, extensive.
- Charge density ρ_eff [C/m³ or C/m²]: can be positive or negative,
  depends on trap charge state, Fermi level, temperature.
- Interface trap density D_it [cm⁻² eV⁻¹]: a spectral density over energy.
- The MVP must not conflate these quantities.

**Expert 4 (Quantum):** For spin qubits, the relevant quantity is not
bulk charge density but local electrostatic potential disorder δφ, which
arises from spatially varying trap charge. The chain
C_def → trap density perturbation → δρ_eff → δφ is physically motivated
but each link introduces assumptions.

---

## Item 3 — Risk of Direct C_def → Charge Mapping

**Expert 1:** C_def is a dimensionless proxy (tier TX). Mapping it directly
to charge (ρ_eff ∝ C_def) assigns physical meaning to a quantity explicitly
defined as non-physical. This is the primary overclaim risk.

**Expert 5 (Engineering):** The quality gates must enforce that:
- C_def remains classified TX in the registry.
- Any mapping function h(C_def) must be documented as uncalibrated.
- No output should be presented as "predicted charge density."

---

## Item 4 — Necessity of δρ_eff

**Expert 2 (Numerics):** Poisson's equation ∇·(ε∇φ) = −ρ_eff requires handling reference states. If ρ_eff has a large uniform
component, the potential φ is dominated by a quadratic background that
masks the physically interesting spatial variation. Using
δρ_eff = ρ_eff − ⟨ρ_eff⟩ suppresses this uniform offset and focuses on the perturbation, supporting charge-neutral solvability. The potential φ itself still retains a gauge freedom.

**Expert 1:** This is exactly what Boussinesq does: subtract the
hydrostatic reference to isolate the buoyancy perturbation.

**Council consensus:** δρ_eff (mean-subtracted or reference-subtracted)
is mandatory for v0.4.

---

## Item 5 — Reference State Subtraction

**Expert 2:** Two options:
- **Spatial mean:** δρ_eff = ρ_eff_raw − ⟨ρ_eff_raw⟩_spatial. Simple,
  reference-consistent, numerically stable.
- **Uniform reference:** δρ_eff = ρ_eff_raw − ρ_ref with ρ_ref from a
  homogeneous case. More physical but requires defining the reference.

**Expert 3:** For the MVP, spatial mean subtraction is sufficient.
Uniform reference subtraction is a v0.5+ refinement.

**Council consensus:** v0.4 should use spatial mean subtraction.

---

## Item 6 — General Poisson Form

**Expert 2:** The general form ∇·(ε∇φ) = −ρ_eff handles spatially varying
permittivity. The simplified form ∇²φ = −ρ_eff/ε assumes homogeneous ε.

**Expert 3:** Silicon permittivity is essentially homogeneous (ε_Si ≈ 11.7 ε₀)
far from interfaces. The homogeneous simplification is acceptable for the
MVP if documented as such.

**Council consensus:** General form in specifications and docs. Homogeneous
simplification allowed in implementation with explicit documentation.

---

## Item 7 — Option C Advantages and Risks

**Expert 3:** Option C (trap/interface occupancy proxy from v0.3.5) is
physically grounded in interface trap physics. D_it provides a natural
density scale. The simplified form reduces to Option A with a physically
motivated scale factor.

**Risk:** Without Fermi level self-consistency, trap occupancy is assumed
constant or parametrically controlled, which limits physical fidelity.

---

## Item 8 — Option C-B Proposal

**Expert 1:** Option C-B combines Option C with the Boussinesq perturbation
philosophy:

```
θ(x,y) = [T(x,y) − T_ref] / ΔT_ref

h(C_def) = C_def / C_sat   (or another bounded monotonic function)

D_it_eff(x,y) = D_it_ref · [1 + λT·θ(x,y) + λC·h(C_def(x,y))]

ρ_eff_raw(x,y) = q · D_it_eff(x,y) · I(T)

δρ_eff(x,y) = ρ_eff_raw(x,y) − ⟨ρ_eff_raw⟩

∇·(ε∇φ) = −δρ_eff
```

**Expert 2:** The linearized form is well-posed for small λT, λC. The
mean subtraction ensures ∫δρ_eff dA ≈ 0, which is consistent with
charge neutrality at the domain scale.

**Expert 4:** This captures the qualitative physics: thermal gradients and
defect heterogeneity perturb trap density and therefore local charge. The
resulting δφ would represent electrostatic disorder at the level needed
for a methodological demonstration.

**Expert 5:** From an engineering perspective, Option C-B is testable:
- Manufactured solutions exist for Poisson with known sources.
- The closure function is invertible and unit-testable.
- Parameter sensitivity analysis extends naturally from v0.3.

## Item 10 — Second Micro-Refinement Review

**Expert 1 (Dr. A - Materials):** The structural homology is valid, but linearization is dangerous. It is only valid in the strict small-perturbation limit. We must not claim physical equivalence to Boussinesq fluid dynamics.

**Expert 3 (Dra. B - Devices):** The causal chain C_def → traps → occupancy → charge is correct. However, real occupancy depends on φ. The v0.4 MVP must explicitly declare this a static decoupled approximation. Self-consistency is out of scope.

**Expert 2 (Dr. C - Numerics):** Mean subtraction is numerically valuable; background removal significantly improves solvability. Furthermore, N_ref and λ terms must be demonstrative numerical scales, avoiding any fitting or calibration claims without data.
