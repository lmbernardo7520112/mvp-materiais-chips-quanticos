## Deliberation

### 1. Semiconductor Device Physicist

> The evolution from constant D_it to D_it(E) is physically well-motivated.
> Real Si/SiO₂ interfaces exhibit U-shaped D_it distributions across the
> bandgap. The constant approximation is acceptable for order-of-magnitude
> bookkeeping but masks energy-dependent structure.
>
> **Recommendation:** P1 piecewise-constant is the right next step. It
> captures energy dependence without requiring a specific functional form
> that we cannot justify without experimental data. P2 Gaussian should
> wait until there is a physical basis for choosing E₀ and σ_E.
>
> **Vote:** P1 next.

### 2. Computational Materials Scientist

> P1 is numerically trivial (sum of rectangles) and preserves the
> dimensional chain. The implementation risk is low because the integration
> degenerates to P0 with a single bin. This makes regression testing
> straightforward.
>
> P2 and P3 add complexity without proportional physical insight at this
> stage. They should remain as future candidates once P1 is validated.
>
> **Vote:** P1 next.

### 3. Applied Mathematician

> The piecewise-constant approximation is a zeroth-order quadrature rule.
> It converges to the true integral as bin width → 0. The error bound is
> well-characterized: O(h × max|D_it'|) per bin, where h is the bin width.
>
> Implementation must enforce: (a) ordered energy edges, (b) non-negative
> densities, (c) no gaps or overlaps in the partition.
>
> **Vote:** P1 next. P3 triangular could serve as a first-order
> convergence test in the future.

### 4. Research Software Engineer

> P1 is the most testable profile. The RED tests are clear: verify sum of
> rectangles, verify P0 regression, verify edge ordering, verify
> non-negativity. The API surface is small: accept a list of (D_i, E_i)
> pairs and return N_it.
>
> I would recommend a `compute_nit_piecewise(densities, edges)` function
> alongside the existing `compute_nit_areal_density`. Both should produce
> N_it in m⁻², and the downstream σ_eff chain should be agnostic to which
> function generated N_it.
>
> **Vote:** P1 next.

### 5. Scientific Governance Auditor

> ADR-010 correctly scopes the proposal. The "What ADR-010 Does NOT
> Authorize" section is essential. I verify that:
>
> - No code changes are proposed in v0.5.3.
> - The P1 recommendation is documented, not implemented.
> - The C1/C2 boundary is preserved.
> - The non-calibration statement is explicit.
>
> **Vote:** P1 next, with strict enforcement of documentation-only scope.

### 6. Skeptical External Reviewer

> **Objection 1:** Piecewise-constant D_it(E) with arbitrary bin values
> could be tuned to match any target σ_eff, creating a false sense of
> physical fidelity. Without experimental constraints on the bin values,
> this is just curve-fitting with extra steps.
>
> **Response:** Valid concern. Mitigation: all benchmark scenarios must
> use literature-anchored ranges for D_i values (same T2/T3 tiers as
> v0.5.2). The CSV metadata must continue to declare `not_calibrated`
> and `device_prediction_claimed = False`. The risk matrix (R8) explicitly
> flags overfitting without data.
>
> **Objection 2:** Adding energy bins to D_it(E) may be perceived as
> "validating" the model's physics, especially if figures show bin-resolved
> σ_eff contributions. This risks scope creep toward C2.
>
> **Response:** Valid concern. Mitigation: future implementation must NOT
> produce bin-resolved σ_eff contributions. The output remains a single
> N_it scalar per scenario. The C1/C2 boundary is that σ_eff is the
> terminal output; ρ_eff/t_eff/solver coupling remain blocked. The
> acceptance gates (G9–G11) enforce this.
>
> **Vote:** P1 next, with mitigations accepted.

## Council Decision

1. **P0** remains as baseline and regression reference.
2. **P1 piecewise-constant** is approved as the next implementation candidate.
3. **P2 Gaussian** and **P3 Triangular** are deferred to future releases.
4. **C2 remains blocked.** No ρ_eff, t_eff, solver coupling, or physical φ.
5. **Implementation** must follow RED-GREEN-REFACTOR with dedicated ADR acceptance.
6. **Mitigations** from the Skeptical Reviewer are incorporated into the risk matrix.
