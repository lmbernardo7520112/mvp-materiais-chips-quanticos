## Expert 1 — Research Software Engineer

**Vote: Accept.**

The ADR is well-structured. C1 scope is clear: three pure functions
(`convert_dit_to_si`, `compute_nit`, `compute_sigma_eff`) with no solver
dependency. The 8 future RED tests are concrete and testable. The TDD
plan specifies expected ModuleNotFoundError as RED state. This is ready
for implementation planning.

---

## Expert 2 — Semiconductor Device Physicist

**Vote: Accept.**

The D_it → N_it → σ_eff chain follows standard MOS interface physics.
The conversion factor 6.2415×10²² is correct (1/q_e × 10⁴). Leaving
δE_window and sign convention as explicit parameters is the right
approach — in real devices these depend on Fermi level position and
trap type, which C1 does not model.

---

## Expert 3 — Computational Materials Scientist

**Vote: Accept.**

C1 stops at σ_eff [C/m²], which is a well-defined surface quantity.
The explicit exclusion of ρ_eff and t_eff prevents the dimensional
confusion that plagues simplified charge models. The benchmark-before-C2
requirement adds a safety net for future solver coupling.

---

## Expert 4 — Applied Mathematician / PDE Specialist

**Vote: Accept.**

C1 is mathematically trivial (scalar arithmetic), which means the RED
tests are straightforward and the implementation risk is minimal. The
real complexity begins at C2 (PDE source term coupling). Accepting C1
now allows us to build and validate the upstream chain independently.

---

## Expert 5 — Numerical Methods Specialist

**Vote: Accept.**

I confirm that C1 has zero impact on the solver. No convergence risk,
no condition number change, no stiffness. The manufactured-solution
benchmark requirement for C2 is essential — I recommend specifying it
as a formal gate in the C2 ADR (future, not now).

---

## Expert 6 — Skeptical External Reviewer

**Vote: Accept, with two objections.**

### Objection 1: C1 produces no simulation output

C1 computes σ_eff but never feeds it into a PDE. The simulation output
(φ, convergence metrics) remains unchanged. What is the scientific
deliverable of C1?

**Response (Expert 2):** The deliverable is a validated dimensional
chain with SI units. This is preparation, not a simulation result.
But it is *testable* preparation — every step can be verified with
unit tests. The alternative (skipping to C2) would couple an untested
chain to the solver.

### Objection 2: Will C1 delay C2 indefinitely?

The project has spent v0.4.7–v0.4.10 on governance without producing
new physics code. Is C1 acceptance just another delay tactic?

**Response (Expert 1):** C1 acceptance is the *exit* from governance-only
releases. After v0.4.10, the next step is v0.5.0 RED — actual test code.
The methodology audit (v0.4.8) explicitly flagged that governance was
outpacing science. C1 is the course correction.

**Expert 6 (accepting):** I accept. The trajectory from v0.4.10 to
v0.5.0 RED is concrete and breaks the documentation-only pattern.
