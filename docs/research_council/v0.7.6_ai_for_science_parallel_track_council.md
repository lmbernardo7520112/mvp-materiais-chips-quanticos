## 1. AI-for-Science Research Lead

**Should AI-for-Science enter now?** Yes, but strictly as feasibility documentation.

**In what form?** AIFS-0 (conceptual) and AIFS-1 (analytic benchmark design) only. No code, no dependencies.

**PINNs now?** No. PINNs require a classical baseline to benchmark against. C3 is not yet implemented.

**Contamination avoidance:** Strict namespace isolation. AI track cannot import from classical modules without explicit gate.

**Dependencies blocked:** All ML frameworks must remain blocked until ADR acceptance and dependency decision.

**Minimum future benchmark:** 1D Poisson with known analytic solution. Error must be quantified against exact solution before any project physics coupling.

**Prohibited:** Solver replacement, physical interpretation from ML, calibration claims.

**Recommendation:** Approve AIFS-A. Block all implementation.

## 2. PINN Specialist

**Should AI-for-Science enter now?** Documentation only is prudent.

**In what form?** Feasibility notes describing how PINNs could approximate PDEs in the project domain.

**PINNs now?** No. PINNs require careful loss function design, residual point sampling, and boundary condition enforcement. None of this infrastructure exists. Implementing prematurely would produce misleading results.

**Contamination avoidance:** PINNs must never replace the classical solver. They should only emulate it after the classical solver produces verified baselines.

**Dependencies blocked:** PyTorch/JAX are necessary for PINNs but should not be added without governance.

**Minimum future benchmark:** Manufactured solution for 2D Poisson. Must demonstrate convergence with mesh refinement analog (increasing collocation points).

**Prohibited:** Claiming PINN accuracy without error quantification against classical solver.

**Recommendation:** Approve documentation. Block implementation.

## 3. Numerical PDE Specialist

**Should AI-for-Science enter now?** Only as documentation.

**In what form?** Analysis of which PDEs in the project could theoretically be approximated by neural methods.

**PINNs now?** Absolutely not. The classical solver (C3) is not yet coupled. There is no baseline to compare against. PINNs without baselines are scientifically meaningless.

**Contamination avoidance:** Classical solver must be the sole source of truth. Any future ML method must be validated against it, not the other way around.

**Dependencies blocked:** All. The project's numerical stack (numpy, scipy) is sufficient for the classical path.

**Minimum future benchmark:** Analytic solution benchmark. The first PINN test must solve a problem with a known exact solution.

**Prohibited:** Replacing finite-difference solver. Claiming neural convergence without convergence analysis.

**Recommendation:** Approve documentation only. Classical path is the priority.

## 9. AI Safety / AI Failure Mode Reviewer

**Should AI-for-Science enter now?** Documentation only.

**In what form?** Failure mode taxonomy specific to AI-for-Science in this project.

**PINNs now?** No. Key AI failure modes have not been addressed:
- Training on synthetic data may encode artifacts.
- Low loss does not imply physical correctness.
- Neural networks can satisfy boundary conditions while being wrong in the interior.
- Stochastic training breaks reproducibility.

**Contamination avoidance:** AI failure mode review must be mandatory before any AI track code.

**Dependencies blocked:** All.

**Minimum future benchmark:** Must include failure case analysis, not just success metrics.

**Prohibited:** Treating training loss as physical validation. Optimistic AI claims without evidence.

**Recommendation:** Approve documentation. Block implementation until failure modes are governed.

## 10. Skeptical External Reviewer

### Objections

**Objection 1: AlphaFold analogy may create hype.**
AlphaFold solved a well-defined prediction problem with massive experimental training data. This project has zero experimental data. The analogy is misleading and may create false expectations about what AI-for-Science can achieve here.
> **Response:** Agreed. ADR-014 explicitly states that AlphaFold-style claims are blocked. Any external AI-for-Science reference is classified as "high-level conceptual context, not project evidence."

**Objection 2: PINNs may underperform classical solvers.**
For well-posed elliptic PDEs on structured grids, classical finite-difference methods are typically faster and more accurate than PINNs. The overhead of neural network training may not be justified.
> **Response:** Agreed. This is why the classical track remains canonical. PINNs are only considered for future scenarios where parametric sweeps or inverse problems might benefit from learned approximations, and only after classical baselines exist.

**Objection 3: ML dependencies may bloat CI.**
PyTorch, TensorFlow, or JAX would add significant install time, disk usage, and potential version conflicts to CI.
> **Response:** Agreed. Dependency governance skill blocks all ML packages. Future addition requires dependency decision brief with CI impact quantification.

**Objection 4: Surrogates may hide physical errors.**
A surrogate model trained on classical solver outputs inherits all errors of the training data. If the classical solver has bugs or unphysical boundary conditions, the surrogate will reproduce them faithfully.
> **Response:** Agreed. This is why classical solver validation (C3 + benchmarks) must precede any surrogate training. The AIFS evidence levels enforce this ordering.

**Objection 5: Training without data may be meaningless.**
PINNs can be trained without experimental data by minimizing PDE residuals. However, this only verifies consistency with the PDE, not physical correctness. If the PDE model itself is wrong or incomplete, the PINN will faithfully reproduce the wrong physics.
> **Response:** Agreed. This is why AIFS-2 (isolated toy prototype) requires an analytic benchmark first, not project physics.

**Objection 6: Solver replacement may destroy traceability.**
Replacing a transparent finite-difference solver with a neural network eliminates the ability to trace physical behavior to specific numerical operations.
> **Response:** Agreed. ADR-014 explicitly blocks solver replacement. The classical solver remains canonical and required.

**Objection 7: AI track may distract from C3 classical roadmap.**
Opening an AI-for-Science track may divert attention and effort from the critical C3 classical solver coupling, which is the next scientific milestone.
> **Response:** Agreed. This is why v0.7.6 is documentation-only. C3 classical coupling (v0.8.0+) takes priority. The AI track is explicitly non-blocking.

**Recommendation:** Approve documentation-only feasibility. Block all implementation. Keep classical track canonical. Ensure C3 is not delayed.

---

## Council Decision

**Unanimous (10/10): Approve documentation-only parallel feasibility (AIFS-A).**

- Block ML implementation.
- Block ML dependencies.
- Keep classical track canonical.
- C3 classical coupling takes priority.
- Future AI-for-Science implementation requires: accepted ADR, dependency decision, analytic benchmarks, RED tests, reproducibility plan, and human approval.
