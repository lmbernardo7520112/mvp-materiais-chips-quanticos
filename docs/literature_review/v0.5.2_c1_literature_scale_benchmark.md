# v0.5.2 C1 Literature Scale Benchmark

## Purpose

Contextualize the numerical scales of C1 surface-density bookkeeping
(σ_eff) against plausible literature ranges for interface trap density
(D_it), energy window (ΔE), and occupancy (f_occ) in silicon-based
quantum devices. This review does NOT calibrate the model and does NOT
validate device-level predictions.

## Literature Themes

### 1. Interface Trap Density (D_it)

The density of interface states at Si/SiO₂ interfaces is a critical
parameter governing charge noise in MOS quantum dot devices. Values
depend on fabrication quality, oxidation conditions, and post-processing
(e.g., forming gas anneals).

- **High-quality Si/SiO₂ (passivated):** D_it ~ 10¹⁰ eV⁻¹·cm⁻²
  near midgap. This is considered excellent for spin-qubit applications.
- **Standard CMOS-grade Si/SiO₂:** D_it ~ 10¹⁰–10¹¹ eV⁻¹·cm⁻².
  Typical range for research-grade MOS quantum dots.
- **High-defect / unpassivated interfaces:** D_it ~ 10¹¹–10¹² eV⁻¹·cm⁻².
  Represents poor or degraded interfaces.
- **Si/SiGe heterostructures:** D_it ~ 5×10⁹–5×10¹⁰ eV⁻¹·cm⁻² at
  buried interfaces (modeled values).

**Key references:**
- Sze & Ng, *Physics of Semiconductor Devices*, 3rd ed., Wiley (2006):
  classical reference for D_it characterization methods and typical values.
- Connors et al., "Charge-noise spectroscopy of Si/SiGe quantum dots via
  dynamically-decoupled exchange oscillations," *Nature Physics* 18, 325–329
  (2022): charge noise spectroscopy in silicon spin qubits.
- Paquelet Wuetz et al., "Reducing charge noise in quantum dots by using
  thin silicon quantum wells," *Nature Communications* 14, 1385 (2023):
  interface engineering for noise reduction in Si/SiGe.

### 2. Charge Noise in Silicon Spin Qubits

Charge noise is the primary decoherence source in spin qubits, arising
from two-level fluctuators (TLFs) at the semiconductor–dielectric
interface. Typical charge noise power spectral density at 1 Hz:

- **Optimized devices:** S_ε ~ 0.1–0.3 μeV/√Hz
- **Typical devices:** S_ε ~ 0.3–1.0 μeV/√Hz
- **High-noise devices:** S_ε > 1.0 μeV/√Hz

The noise spectrum consistently follows 1/f^α with α ≈ 1.

**Key references:**
- Yoneda et al., "A quantum-dot spin qubit with coherence limited by
  charge noise and fidelity higher than 99.9%," *Nature Nanotechnology*
  13, 102–106 (2018).
- Kranz et al., "Exploiting a Single-Crystal Environment to Minimize
  the Charge Noise on Qubits in Silicon," *Advanced Materials* 32,
  2003361 (2020).

### 3. Two-Level Fluctuators (TLFs)

TLFs are microscopic charge traps at or near the interface that switch
between two discrete charge states. They are the microscopic origin of
1/f charge noise.

- Spatial density: related to D_it but includes sub-surface traps.
- Switching rates: thermally activated, bias-dependent.
- Impact: individual TLFs create random telegraph noise (RTN) in
  charge sensors; ensemble creates 1/f noise.

**Key references:**
- Paladino et al., "1/f noise: Implications for solid-state quantum
  information," *Reviews of Modern Physics* 86, 361 (2014).
- Culcer & Zimmerman, "Dephasing of Si spin qubits due to charge
  noise," *Applied Physics Letters* 102, 232108 (2013).

### 4. Materials / Interface / Gate Stack

The dominant interfaces for silicon spin qubits:

- **SiMOS (Si/SiO₂):** Direct oxide interface; higher D_it but
  simpler fabrication. IMEC, Intel platforms.
- **Si/SiGe heterostructures:** Buried quantum well; lower D_it at
  the Si/SiGe interface but remote charges from surface oxides.
- **Gate stacks:** Al₂O₃/SiO₂ bilayers, HfO₂ — affect D_it through
  fixed charge and border traps.

### 5. Limitations of Literature-Scale Benchmarking

Literature D_it values:

- Are measured on test capacitors, not on actual qubit devices.
- Depend strongly on measurement technique (conductance, Terman, DLTS).
- Vary with energy position in the bandgap (U-shaped distribution).
- Are reported at room temperature; cryogenic behavior differs.
- Do not directly map to charge noise magnitude without modeling.

Therefore, using literature D_it ranges as benchmark anchors is valid
for dimensional sanity checks, but does NOT constitute calibration or
device-level prediction.

## Non-Calibration Statement

> **These ranges are literature-scale anchors and sanity benchmarks only.
> They do not calibrate the model and do not validate device-level
> predictions. The C1 module computes σ_eff as a dimensional bookkeeping
> exercise. Converting σ_eff to physical charge density (ρ_eff) or
> coupling it to the Poisson solver requires C2 governance (future ADR).**
