## A — Point Defects in Silicon

### A1. Sinno et al. (1999)

- **Ref:** T. Sinno, E. Dornberger, W. von Ammon, R.A. Brown, F. Dupret,
  "Defect engineering of Czochralski single-crystal silicon,"
  *Materials Science and Engineering: R: Reports*, 28(5-6), 149–198.
- **Classification:** equation-source, parameter-source
- **Key facts:**
  - Vacancy formation energy: E_f^V ≈ 3.6–4.0 eV.
  - Vacancy migration energy: E_m^V ≈ 0.2–0.5 eV.
  - Interstitial migration energy: E_m^I ≈ 0.7–1.5 eV.
  - Equilibrium vacancy concentration: C_V^eq ∝ exp(−E_f / k_B T).
  - Recombination barrier: 0.3–1.0 eV (Frenkel pair recombination).
  - Void nucleation window: ~1100°C (Voronkov criterion).
- **Decision of use:** Parameter-source for E_D, E_R, T_G ranges.
  Values are literature-inspired, not calibrated.
- **Limitation:** Czochralski-specific; may not apply to epitaxial or
  SOI processes relevant to quantum devices.

### A2. Voronkov (1982, 1999)

- **Ref:** V.V. Voronkov, "The mechanism of swirl defects formation in
  silicon," *J. Crystal Growth*, 59(3), 625–643 (1982). And subsequent
  reviews.
- **Classification:** parameter-source, review/context-source
- **Key facts:**
  - Critical V/G ratio determines vacancy vs. interstitial dominance.
  - Void nucleation temperature: ~1100–1150°C (~1373–1423 K).
  - Temperature window for defect aggregation: ~1000–1150°C (~1273–1423 K).
- **Decision of use:** Literature reports void nucleation at ~1100°C
  (~1373 K). The MVP default T_G = 1100 K is a **toy value** that does
  **not** correspond to the Voronkov temperature. The literature range
  (~1373 K) is recorded as a candidate for future calibration.
- **Limitation:** Applies to CZ growth; quantum device substrates may
  use different processes.

### A3. Bracht (2000)

- **Ref:** H. Bracht, "Diffusion mechanisms and intrinsic point-defect
  properties in silicon," *MRS Bulletin*, 25(6), 22–27.
- **Classification:** parameter-source
- **Key facts:**
  - Self-diffusion in Si mediated by vacancies and interstitials.
  - D₀ for vacancy diffusion: ~1e-5 to 1e-3 m²/s (pre-exponential).
  - Activation energies for self-diffusion: ~3.0–4.5 eV (total).
  - Migration alone: 0.2–0.5 eV for vacancies.
- **Decision of use:** Bounds D₀_def and E_D ranges.
- **Limitation:** Bulk self-diffusion; near-surface or interface diffusion
  may differ significantly.

## B — Interface Traps / Oxide-Semiconductor Disorder

### B1. Fleetwood (2018)

- **Ref:** D.M. Fleetwood, "Border traps and bias-temperature
  instabilities in MOS devices," *Microelectronics Reliability*, 80, 1–23.
- **Classification:** device-impact-source, review/context-source
- **Key facts:**
  - Border traps at Si/SiO₂ interface: density ~1e10 to 1e12 cm⁻² eV⁻¹.
  - Trap energy levels distributed across Si bandgap.
  - Charge capture/emission time constants: thermally activated.
  - Direct impact on threshold voltage instability and 1/f noise.
- **Inference:** Interface trap density correlates with charge disorder
  relevant to qubit variability. This is **not** modeled in the MVP.
- **Limitation:** MOSFET context; quantum dot geometry differs.

### B2. Sze & Ng (2007)

- **Ref:** S.M. Sze, K.K. Ng, *Physics of Semiconductor Devices*, 3rd ed.,
  Wiley.
- **Classification:** review/context-source, parameter-source
- **Key facts:**
  - D_it (interface trap density): 1e10–1e12 cm⁻² eV⁻¹ for good Si/SiO₂.
  - Trap-assisted tunneling and Shockley-Read-Hall recombination.
  - Trap occupancy follows Fermi-Dirac statistics.
- **Decision of use:** Not used for calibration. Provides context for
  physical meaning of potential ρ_eff → D_it mapping.
- **Limitation:** Bulk planar devices; quantum dot confinement not addressed.

## C — Charge Disorder / Charge Noise in Silicon Quantum Dots

### C1. Connors et al. (2022)

- **Ref:** E.J. Connors et al., "Charge-noise spectroscopy of
  Si/SiGe quantum dots via dynamically-decoupled exchange oscillations,"
  *Nature Communications*, 13, 940.
- **Classification:** device-impact-source
- **Key facts:**
  - Charge noise spectral density: S_ε ~ 1–10 μeV²/Hz at 1 Hz.
  - 1/f^α spectrum with α ≈ 0.6–1.0.
  - Attributed to two-level fluctuators (TLFs) at interfaces.
  - Noise amplitude correlates with interface quality.
- **Hypothesis:** Spatial distribution of TLFs could, in principle, be
  related to a defect density field — but this is **not** the approach
  taken in the MVP.
- **Limitation:** Experimental; no direct mapping to C_def exists.

### C2. Struck et al. (2020)

- **Ref:** T. Struck et al., "Low-frequency noise in quantum dots,"
  *Applied Physics Reviews*, 7, 011307.
- **Classification:** review/context-source, device-impact-source
- **Key facts:**
  - Charge noise limits single-qubit gate fidelity.
  - Sources: oxide traps, dangling bonds, mobile ions.
  - Spatial correlation of noise depends on defect distribution.
  - Noise PSD depends on distance between qubit and defect.
- **Inference:** A spatial defect density field (like C_def) is conceptually
  related to the microscopic sources of charge noise. However, the
  functional mapping is complex and **not** attempted in v0.3.
- **Limitation:** No quantitative prescription for C_def → noise mapping.

### C3. Zimmerman et al. (2007)

- **Ref:** N.M. Zimmerman, W.H. Huber, B. Simonds, E. Hourdakis,
  A. Fujiwara, Y. Ono, Y. Takahashi, H. Inokawa, M. Furlan, M.W. Keller,
  "Charge offset stability in tunable-barrier Si single-electron tunneling
  devices," *Applied Physics Letters*, 90, 033507 (2007).
  **Metadata note:** year and article number corrected from earlier draft.
- **Classification:** device-impact-source
- **Key facts:**
  - Charge offset drift: ~0.01–0.1 e over hours.
  - Attributed to slow trap dynamics near quantum dot.
  - Temperature dependence suggests thermally activated process.
- **Decision of use:** Motivates temperature-dependent kinetics in the
  defect model. Does **not** calibrate parameters.

## D — Temperature-Activated Defect/Trap Kinetics

### D1. Fahey et al. (1989)

- **Ref:** P.M. Fahey, P.B. Griffin, J.D. Plummer, "Point defects and
  dopant diffusion in silicon," *Reviews of Modern Physics*, 61(2), 289–384.
- **Classification:** equation-source, parameter-source
- **Key facts:**
  - Arrhenius diffusivity: D(T) = D₀ exp(−E_a / k_B T).
  - Defect generation and recombination follow mass-action kinetics.
  - Temperature windows for defect aggregation are species-specific.
- **Decision of use:** Equation-source for the Arrhenius form used in
  defect_kinetics.py. Parameter ranges for D₀ and E_a.
- **Limitation:** Dopant-specific; generic defect kinetics are simplified.

### D2. Pichler (2004)

- **Ref:** P. Pichler, *Intrinsic Point Defects, Impurities, and Their
  Diffusion in Silicon*, Springer.
- **Classification:** parameter-source, review/context-source
- **Key facts:**
  - Comprehensive tabulation of defect diffusion parameters in Si.
  - Vacancy D₀: 1e-5 to 1e-3 m²/s; E_m: 0.2–0.5 eV.
  - Interstitial D₀: ~1e-4 to 1e-2 m²/s; E_m: 0.7–1.5 eV.
  - Recombination rate constants: thermally activated.
- **Decision of use:** Primary reference for parameter tier classification.

## E — Implications for Future Electrostatic Coupling

### E1. Hypothetical C_def → ρ_eff Pathway

- **Classification:** not-used-for-calibration
- **Hypothesis:** A mapping from C_def (dimensionless defect proxy) to
  ρ_eff (effective charge density) would require:
  1. Charge state assignment per defect type (qV, qI, qT).
  2. Spatial integration with Poisson equation: ∇·(ε∇φ) = −ρ_eff.
     (∇²φ = −ρ_eff/ε is the homogeneous-permittivity simplification.)
  3. Knowledge of Fermi level position and trap occupancy.
- **Fact:** None of these steps are implemented in v0.3.
- **Inference:** The simplest candidate mapping (ρ_eff ∝ q · C_def) is
  physically naive but mathematically tractable as a v0.4 starting point.
- **Limitation:** Without Poisson solver, Fermi level self-consistency,
  or charge state models, any mapping is speculative.
- **Decision:** Defer implementation to v0.4. Document options in
  decision brief.
