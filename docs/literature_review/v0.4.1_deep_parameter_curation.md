# Deep Parameter Literature Review — v0.4.1

> **Date:** 2026-05-10
> **Status:** Curation-only — no calibration performed
> **Scope:** Si/SiO₂ and Si/SiGe heterostructures for CMOS spin-qubit context
> **ADR:** ADR-007 (Accepted, v0.4 scope)

## 1. Purpose

This review audits every parameter in the MVP v0.4 computational pipeline
against primary literature, classifying evidence tiers and identifying
the gap between demonstrative/toy values and literature-supported ranges.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This is a **curation** exercise. No parameter is promoted to "calibrated"
> status. The MVP remains demonstrative. This review exists to inform future
> calibration decisions and to prevent overclaims.

## 2. Material Stack Context

The relevant material system is:

| Layer | Material | Role |
|-------|----------|------|
| Gate oxide | SiO₂ (thermally grown) | Dielectric / interface source |
| Channel | Si (intrinsic or lightly doped) | Quantum dot host |
| Barrier | Si₀.₇Ge₀.₃ (typical) | Heterostructure confinement |
| Substrate | Si (001) | Mechanical support |

This stack is representative of Si/SiGe spin-qubit architectures
(Zwanenburg et al., 2013; Burkard et al., 2023).

## 3. Physical Constants (CONST tier)

| Symbol | Name | Value | Unit | Source | Exactness |
|--------|------|-------|------|--------|----------|
| q | Elementary charge | 1.602176634×10⁻¹⁹ | C | CODATA 2018 | EXACT (SI 2019 defining constant) |
| k_B | Boltzmann constant | 1.380649×10⁻²³ | J/K | CODATA 2018 | EXACT (SI 2019 defining constant) |
| k_B | Boltzmann constant | 8.617333262×10⁻⁵ | eV/K | Derived | EXACT (derived from exact q, k_B) |
| ε₀ | Vacuum permittivity | 8.8541878128×10⁻¹² | F/m | CODATA 2018 | DERIVED (not a defining constant) |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **q** and **k_B** are exact by the 2019 SI redefinition (defining constants).
> **ε₀** is NOT a defining constant of the 2019 SI. It is derived from
> ε₀ = 1/(μ₀c²), where c is exact but μ₀ now carries experimental uncertainty
> via the fine-structure constant α. The CODATA 2018 recommended value has
> relative uncertainty ~1.5×10⁻¹⁰. For MVP purposes this is negligible, but
> ε₀ must not be described as "exact" in the same sense as q or k_B.

## 4. Semiconductor / Interface Parameters

### 4.1 Relative Permittivity (ε_r)

| Material | ε_r (300 K) | Source | Type |
|----------|-------------|--------|------|
| Si | 11.7 | Ioffe Institute; Sze & Ng (2007) | TEXTBOOK |
| SiO₂ | 3.9 | Ioffe Institute; Sze & Ng (2007) | TEXTBOOK |
| Si₁₋ₓGeₓ | 11.7 + 4.5x | Ioffe Institute | REVIEW |

- **Temperature dependence:** Si ε_r decreases weakly with T. At cryogenic
  temperatures (< 4 K), ε_r ≈ 11.4–11.7 depending on doping.
- **MVP status:** Not used directly (ε=1.0 demonstrative). Would require
  conversion via ε = ε_r · ε₀ for physical solver.
- **Proposed tier:** T2 (literature-supported candidate range).

### 4.2 Interface Trap Density (D_it)

| Range | Unit | Material | Source | Source Role |
|-------|------|----------|--------|------------|
| 10¹⁰–10¹² | eV⁻¹ cm⁻² | Si/SiO₂ | Fleetwood (2018); Sze & Ng (2007) | PARAMETER_ANCHOR |
| 10⁹–10¹¹ | eV⁻¹ cm⁻² | Si/SiGe (buried) | Connors et al. (2022) | CONTEXT_ONLY |

- **Unit conversion:** 1 eV⁻¹ cm⁻² = 6.242×10²² J⁻¹ m⁻².
- **Relevance:** D_it is a potential physical anchor for a future calibrated
  N_ref. Currently N_ref=1.0 (dimensionless toy). The active N_ref in code
  remains TX — no promotion is performed.
- **Surface vs volume:** D_it is areal (cm⁻²). Converting to volumetric
  requires t_eff: ρ = D_it / t_eff.
- **Connors2022 note:** This source characterizes charge noise in Si/SiGe
  quantum dots and provides device context. It is NOT used as a direct
  numerical anchor for D_it unless explicit D_it extraction is documented.
  Primary D_it anchors are Fleetwood (2018) and Sze & Ng (2007).
- **N_ref mapping (future):** Conceptually, N_ref could be linked to
  q·D_it·t_eff, but this requires unit conversion infrastructure and
  device-specific t_eff. Tier for this mapping concept: T1_candidate.

### 4.3 Effective Thickness (t_eff)

## 5. Effective Charge Closure Parameters (C-B)

### 5.1 Thermal Coupling Weight (λ_T)

- **Current value:** 0.0 (effectively disabled) or 0.1 in script.
- **Physical basis:** Couples thermal fluctuations to charge response.
  No direct literature anchor — this is a model coupling coefficient.
- **Proposed tier:** TX (demonstrative coupling; no physical anchor).

### 5.2 Defect Coupling Weight (λ_C)

- **Current value:** 1.0.
- **Physical basis:** Scales how C_def maps to charge perturbation.
  The functional form h(C_def) = C_def/C_sat is a saturation model.
- **Proposed tier:** TX (demonstrative coupling).

### 5.3 Saturation Concentration (C_sat)

- **Current value:** 1.0 (dimensionless).
- **Physical basis:** If C_def were physical concentration, C_sat would
  represent a maximum trap occupation density. In our model, it serves
  only as a normalization scale.
- **Proposed tier:** TX (dimensionless normalization).

### 5.4 Reference Temperature Parameters (T_ref, δT_ref)

- **Current value:** None (disabled).
- **Physical basis:** Would define the temperature scale for θ(T).
  Literature theta functions typically use T_ref as a process temperature
  (e.g., annealing T ~ 800–1100°C for Si oxidation).
- **Proposed tier:** T1 (form inspired by Arrhenius/Boltzmann, range TBD).

## 6. Defect Reaction-Diffusion Parameters

### 6.1 Defect Diffusion Pre-exponential (D0_def)

**D0 (Arrhenius pre-exponential):**

| Range | Unit | Mechanism | Source | Source Role |
|-------|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| 0.002–10 | cm²/s | Self-diffusion (vacancy) | Bracht et al. (1998) | PARAMETER_ANCHOR |
| 2×10⁻⁷ – 1×10⁻³ | m²/s | Converted to SI (×10⁻⁴) | Derived | — |

> [!WARNING]
> The previous version of this document incorrectly stated the SI conversion
> as 10⁻¹⁴–10⁻⁸ m²/s. The correct conversion of 0.002–10 cm²/s is
> 2×10⁻⁷ – 1×10⁻³ m²/s (multiply by 10⁻⁴).

**D(T) (temperature-dependent effective diffusivity):**

The effective diffusivity D(T) = D0·exp(−E_a/k_BT) can span many orders
of magnitude depending on temperature. At typical process temperatures
(800–1200°C), D(T) for vacancy-mediated self-diffusion in Si is in the
range ~10⁻¹⁶–10⁻¹² m²/s. This must NOT be confused with D0.

- **MVP value:** D0_def = 1.0×10⁻⁸ m²/s.
- **Comparison with literature D0:** The MVP value (10⁻⁸ m²/s) is below
  the Bracht1998 D0 range (2×10⁻⁷ – 10⁻³ m²/s). It may be interpreted
  as a model-scale parameter rather than a direct D0 measurement.
- **Proposed tier:** T1 (literature-inspired scale). Bracht1998 is relevant
  as a primary experimental anchor for silicon self-diffusion, but the MVP
  D0_def remains a model-scale parameter. The current value may be described
  as order-of-magnitude inspired only if the distinction between
  pre-exponential D0 and temperature-dependent D(T) is maintained.

### 6.2 Activation Energy (E_D or E_a)

| Range | Unit | Context | Source |
|-------|------|---------|--------|
| 0.4–0.5 | eV | Vacancy migration energy | Watkins (2000); Sinno et al. (1999) |
| 3.0–5.0 | eV | Total self-diffusion (formation + migration) | Bracht et al. (1998) |

- **MVP value:** 0.5 eV (migration-only proxy).
- **Proposed tier:** T1 (consistent with migration barrier, but
  represents only one component of full diffusion activation).

### 6.3 Generation Rate (G0)

- **MVP value:** 1.0 (toy).
- **Physical basis:** Thermal generation of defects follows
  G ∝ exp(−E_f / k_B T). Our model uses a Gaussian spatial profile.
- **No direct experimental anchor** for the specific functional form.
- **Proposed tier:** T0 (toy).

### 6.4 Recombination Rate (R0)

- **MVP value:** Follows from reaction kinetics.
- **Physical basis:** Defect recombination rate depends on capture
  cross-section and carrier concentration.
- **Proposed tier:** T0 (toy).

## 10. Conclusions

1. **q, k_B** are exact defining constants (CONST_EXACT). **ε₀** is a
   derived constant with negligible uncertainty (CONST_DERIVED).
2. **ε_r** has strong literature support (T2). Not yet used in MVP.
3. **D_it** has well-established ranges (T2). Could inform future N_ref
   mapping, but the active N_ref remains TX in code.
4. **t_eff** is device-specific (T3). Requires experimental input.
5. **Closure parameters** (λ_T, λ_C, C_sat) are demonstrative (TX).
6. **Defect diffusion** D0_def is literature-inspired scale (T1), but the
   distinction between D0 (pre-exponential) and D(T) (effective) must be
   maintained. The MVP value is below the Bracht1998 D0 range.
7. **No parameter should be promoted to calibrated without a dedicated ADR.**
