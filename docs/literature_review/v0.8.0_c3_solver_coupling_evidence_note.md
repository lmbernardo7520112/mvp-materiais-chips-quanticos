# v0.8.0 C3 Solver Coupling Evidence Note

## Purpose

Document the rationale for staged classical solver coupling.

## Sections

### 1. Why C2 must not connect directly to Poisson runtime.
A direct connection bypasses the validation of the charge projection itself. Numerical solvers require explicit domain definitions and boundary settings. Feeding continuous interface sheets directly into a numerical Poisson solver without a proven discrete conservative projection contract can mask discretization errors, violating Specification Driven Development.

### 2. Why conservative projection is the minimum next contract.
A projection step ensures the integral of the projected charge equals the integral of the continuous source. This validates that no charge was lost or artificially created due to the grid mesh.

### 3. Why charge conservation is the primary invariant.
If charge is not strictly conserved, the resulting electrostatic potential field will be wrong. This is a fundamental physical requirement for semiconductor electrostatics. A non-conservative projection acts as an unphysical source or sink.

### 4. Why boundary condition metadata matters.
The solution to the Poisson equation strongly depends on boundary conditions (e.g. gates, ground, floating contacts). C2 must produce an object that explicitly enforces awareness of these boundary settings before Poisson can solve it.

### 5. Why physical phi remains blocked.
Even with a projected charge, a non-self-consistent electrostatic field calculation (without carrier feedback) is a demonstrative approximation. Calling it a physical phi before full C3-D self-consistency invites overclaims.

### 6. Why interface/jump-condition methods remain future high-fidelity path.
Volumetric regularization (C3-A) is numerically simpler but smears the interface mathematically. Advanced methods treat the interface as an internal jump condition (C3-B), yielding a sharper, more accurate electric field at the boundary, requiring deeper modifications to the baseline solver.

### 7. Why AI-for-Science remains parallel and non-authoritative.
The AI-for-Science track is exploratory (ADR-014). It has not passed any benchmark gates. The classical Poisson solver is the canonical model. Injecting AI-for-Science logic into the C3 coupling would contaminate the physically interpretable baseline.

### 8. Future evidence requirements.
Future C3 implementations must provide concrete tests (RED/GREEN) proving that grid spacing, cell area/volume, and charge summation invariances hold exactly.
