## 2. Central Question

> "Is there a published, integrated, open-source pipeline that connects
> thermal process modeling via reaction-diffusion defect dynamics to
> electrostatic perturbation of quantum dots in silicon, in a single
> reproducible codebase?"

**Answer:** No work identified in this directed review integrates these
layers in a single open, test-driven, reproducible codebase. The literature
operates in largely isolated silos:

- **Crystal growth community** models V/I defect dynamics during Czochralski
  pulling but does not connect to quantum devices.
- **Quantum device community** models charge noise and qubit variability
  from static disorder but does not model the thermal history that produced it.
- **TCAD community** solves Poisson-Schrödinger for device electrostatics
  but typically takes defect distributions as input, not as output of a
  process model.

**Our gap:** A reduced, educational, reproducible pipeline bridging
thermal process → defect-like variable → future electrostatic perturbation.
This gap is the raison d'être of the project.

## 3. Seed Reference 0 — Device Target Anchor

### Maurand et al. (2016)

**Full citation:** Maurand, R. et al. "A CMOS silicon spin qubit."
*Nature Communications* 7, 13575 (2016).

**DOI:** [10.1038/ncomms13575](https://doi.org/10.1038/ncomms13575)

**URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/ncomms13575

**Classification:**
- Type: **Device target / CMOS spin-qubit anchor**
- Relevance for motivation: **High**
- Relevance for C_def model: **Medium** (motivates, does not define)
- Relevance for G(T)-R(T,C): **Low** (not a reaction-diffusion article)

### Critical Extraction

1. **Why silicon + CMOS is strategic:** Demonstrates a hole spin qubit
   in a p-type silicon-on-insulator (SOI) transistor with two gates,
   fabricated with an industry-standard CMOS process. Scalability via
   existing foundry infrastructure is the key argument for silicon over
   GaAs or topological platforms.

2. **Device architecture:** The device is a dual-gate p-type SOI
   transistor with an undoped silicon channel. At cryogenic temperatures,
   the two gates define two quantum dots in series: QD1 encodes the
   hole spin qubit; QD2 is used for spin readout. The qubit is a single
   hole spin controlled by phase-tunable microwave modulation exploiting
   intrinsic spin-orbit coupling.

3. **Gate-defined quantum dots:** Gates electrostatically confine charge
   carriers, and the same gates enable control and readout. The potential
   landscape is sensitive to the local electrostatic environment.

4. **Decoherence/disorder mechanisms mentioned or discussed:**
   - ²⁹Si nuclear spins (hyperfine interaction); considered by the
     authors, but they note that the short coherence times observed
     are unlikely explained by ²⁹Si alone
   - Possible charge noise (motivated by complementary literature,
     not directly demonstrated by Maurand et al.)
   - Possible hyperfine coupling with boron dopants diffused from
     source/drain contacts
   - Possible magnetic defects in the dielectric/interface
     (SiO₂–HfSiON gate stack)
   - Device-to-device variability from process variations

   **Note:** Maurand et al. do not explicitly attribute decoherence
   to "charge traps at Si/SiO₂". The connection between charge
   noise/traps and qubit performance is established by complementary
   literature (Martinez & Niquet 2022, Culcer 2009, Shehata 2023).

5. **Why this favors C_def as defect-like variable:** The qubit is
   embedded in a CMOS environment where thermal processing creates
   and distributes defects, traps, and charge disorder. Modeling the
   spatial distribution of such heterogeneities is the bridge from
   process to device.

6. **Why this article MUST NOT calibrate parameters:** Maurand et al.
   report qubit metrics (Rabi frequency, coherence times), not defect
   kinetics. No activation energies, diffusion coefficients, or
   generation/recombination rates are provided for point defects.

7. **Connection to future Poisson v0.4:**
   C_def(x,y,t) → ρ_eff(x,y) = q_eff · C_def(x,y) → ∇·(ε∇φ) = −ρ_eff.
   (Note: ∇²φ = −ρ_eff/ε is the homogeneous-permittivity simplification.)
   The spatial disorder that affects qubit performance originates from
   the process thermal history — exactly the chain we model.

8. **Limitations as a C_def source:** Does not contain reaction-diffusion
   equations, does not model defect dynamics, does not provide kinetic
   parameters. It is the **destination**, not the **equation source**.

> **Verdict:** "A CMOS silicon spin qubit" is the device-target anchor.
> It orients the roadmap but MUST NOT be used for calibrating C_def,
> G(T), or R(T,C).

| ID | Reference | DOI/URL | Relevance |

| A2 | Brown, R.A. et al. "Modelling point defect dynamics in the crystal growth of silicon." *J. Crystal Growth* 142, 166–174 (1994) | https://doi.org/10.1016/0022-0248(94)91240-8 | **High** |
| A3 | Voronkov, V.V. & Falster, R. "Grown-in microdefects, residual vacancies and oxygen precipitation bands in Czochralski silicon." *J. Crystal Growth* 204, 462–474 (1999) | https://doi.org/10.1016/S0022-0248(99)00216-7 | **High** |
| A4 | Sinno, T. et al. "Point defects in crystalline silicon: Fundamentals of their mechanics and thermal equilibria." *Mater. Sci. Eng. R* (1999/2000) | Penn Engineering / ResearchGate | **High** |
| A5 | Evaluation of published point defect parameter sets in 300 mm CZ Si. *Crystals* 11(5), 460 (2021) | https://www.mdpi.com/2073-4352/11/5/460 | **Medium** |

**Schematic equations from Block A (Dornberger, Brown, Voronkov):**

A schematic/reduced form of the V/I reaction-diffusion system used
in crystal growth literature (actual models vary by author and may
include additional terms for equilibrium, clusters, surfaces, oxygen,
and convection):

```
∂C_V/∂t = ∇·(D_V∇C_V) − k_rec(C_V·C_I − C_V^eq·C_I^eq)    [schematic]
∂C_I/∂t = ∇·(D_I∇C_I) − k_rec(C_V·C_I − C_I^eq·C_V^eq)    [schematic]
```

With Arrhenius diffusivities:

```
D_{V,I}(T) = D₀_{V,I} · exp(−E_m/{k_B·T})
```

And equilibrium concentrations:

```
C_{V,I}^eq(T) = A_{V,I} · exp(−E_f/{k_B·T})
```

**Relevance to our project:**
- The equation *structure* (reaction-diffusion + Arrhenius) serves as
  **structural inspiration** for C_def — not as a literal implementation.
- We DO NOT need the full V/I coupled system. A single effective
  defect-like variable C_def captures the qualitative essence at
  reduced complexity.
- Formation energies, migration energies from literature (e.g.,
  E_f^V ≈ 2.6–3.6 eV, E_m^V ≈ 0.2–0.5 eV for vacancies in Si)
  can **inspire** toy parameter orders of magnitude but MUST NOT be
  presented as calibrated for our model.

**Separation of equation source vs parameter source:**
- Dornberger/Brown/Voronkov provide the **form** of the R-D equation
  (equation source).
- Numerical parameter values for v0.3 are **toy/demonstrative** unless
  a specific curated value from peer-reviewed literature is adopted
  (parameter source). No v0.3 parameter may be claimed as calibrated
  for C_def without explicit justification.

### BLOCK B — Reaction-Diffusion for Defects in Semiconductors

| ID | Reference | DOI/URL | Relevance |
|----|-----------|---------|-----------|
| B1 | Dornberger (2001) — same as A1, transient R-D model | See A1 | **High** |
| B2 | Brown (1994) — same as A2, early R-D formulation | See A2 | **High** |
| B3 | TU Dresden — Relating microdefects to growth conditions | https://tu-dresden.de/mn/math/wir/ressourcen/dateien/forschung/publikationen/pdf2003/relating_microdefects_to_growth_conditions.pdf | **Medium** |

**Key insight:** The reaction-diffusion formalism for defects in silicon
is well-established since the 1990s. A common generic form is:

```
∂C/∂t = ∇·(D(T)∇C) + G(T) − R(T,C)
```

Where:
- D(T) follows Arrhenius
- G(T) is thermally activated generation
- R(T,C) is recombination/annihilation (often linear or bimolecular)

Our v0.3 will adopt this **generic form** with a single effective
variable C_def. This is structural inspiration, not literal replication
of any specific V/I model.

### BLOCK C — Interface Traps, Charge Traps, Si/SiO₂

| ID | Reference | DOI/URL | Relevance |
|----|-----------|---------|-----------|
| C1 | Martinez, B. & Niquet, Y.-M. "Variability of electron and hole spin qubits due to interface roughness and charge traps." *Phys. Rev. Applied* 17, 024022 (2022) | https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevApplied.17.024022 | **High** |
| C2 | Massai, L. et al. "Impact of interface traps on charge noise and low-density transport in Ge/SiGe heterostructures." *Commun. Mater.* 5, 150 (2024) | https://doi.org/10.1038/s43246-024-00563-8 | **Medium** |

**Key findings:**
- Martinez & Niquet (2022): **charge traps are significantly more limiting
  than interface roughness** for spin qubit variability. Rabi frequency
  dispersion can exceed one order of magnitude due to traps.
- Massai et al. (2024): interface trap filling drives electrostatic
  disorder, drifts and hysteresis in Ge/SiGe quantum dot arrays.
  Transient low-frequency charge noise is activated by gate voltages.

**Relevance to our project:**
- These papers show that trap/defect density **directly impacts** qubit
  performance — motivating why C_def matters.
- They do NOT provide reaction-diffusion kinetics for trap generation.
- They operate at the **device level** (v0.4/v0.5 in our roadmap), not
  at the process level (v0.3).

### BLOCK D — Si/SiGe, Heterostructures, Interface Disorder

| ID | Reference | DOI/URL | Relevance |
|----|-----------|---------|-----------|
| D1 | Peña, L.F. et al. "Modeling Si/SiGe quantum dot variability induced by interface disorder reconstructed from multiperspective microscopy." *npj Quantum Inf.* 10, 33 (2024) | https://doi.org/10.1038/s41534-024-00827-8 | **Medium** |

**Key findings:**
- Valley splitting variability of ~±50% due to alloy disorder.
- Detuning biases of 1–10 meV from uncorrelated interface roughness.
- Atomistic multi-valley effective mass theory used.

**Relevance:** Demonstrates that material heterogeneity at interfaces
directly causes qubit variability. Motivates modeling the origin of
such heterogeneity (our v0.3), though this paper works at the atomistic
device level (our v0.5+).

### BLOCK E — Spin Qubits, Charge Noise, Dephasing

| ID | Reference | DOI/URL | Relevance |
|----|-----------|---------|-----------|
| E1 | Culcer, D. et al. "Dephasing of Si spin qubits due to charge noise." *Appl. Phys. Lett.* 95, 073102 (2009) | https://doi.org/10.1063/1.3194778 | **Medium** |
| E2 | Shehata, M.M.E.K. et al. "Modelling semiconductor spin qubits and their charge noise environment for quantum gate fidelity estimation." *Phys. Rev. B* 108, 045305 (2023) | https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevB.108.045305 | **Medium** |

**Key findings:**
- Culcer (2009): Exchange interaction is Coulombic → sensitive to
  charge noise. Gate errors estimated for Si double quantum dots.
  Dephasing depends on device size and surrounding charge traps.
- Shehata (2023): Co-modeling framework bridging device design to
  qubit energy space. Uses TLF (two-level fluctuator) models. Finds
  inverse correlation between gate errors and confinement frequencies.
  Noise source proximity causes gate fidelity variability.

**Relevance:** Both papers confirm that charge disorder directly
degrades qubit performance. They are **downstream** (v0.5+ in our
roadmap) but validate the motivation for modeling process-generated
heterogeneities (v0.3).

## 6. Answer: Why Maurand et al. is Good but Not Sufficient

- ✅ Good for: technology target, CMOS platform, spin-orbit hole qubits,
  scalability argument, motivating charge noise as performance limiter
- ❌ Not sufficient for: reaction-diffusion equations, defect kinetics,
  activation energies, diffusion coefficients, generation/recombination
  rates, any parameter for C_def
- ⚠️ Must be cited as: **device-target anchor**, never as calibration source

## 7. Gap Identification

The gap our project fills:

```
EXISTING:
  Crystal growth models (Dornberger, Voronkov) → defect distributions
  Device models (Martinez, Shehata, QTCAD) → qubit performance from static disorder

MISSING (our project):
  A single, reduced, reproducible pipeline connecting:
  Thermal process → C_def(x,y,t) → ρ_eff(x,y) → φ(x,y) → proxy confinement

  With:
  - educational/demonstrative parameters
  - full TDD/CI/CD infrastructure
  - versioned, auditable releases
  - explicit limitations at every step
```

No work identified in this directed review integrates these layers in
a single open-source, test-driven, reproducible codebase with the
explicit goal of bridging process modeling to quantum device implications.
This claim is limited to the scope of the present review and does not
constitute an exhaustive survey of all existing work.
