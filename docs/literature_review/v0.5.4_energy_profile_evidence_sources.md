# v0.5.4 Energy Profile Evidence Sources

## Purpose

Evaluate candidate sources for guiding piecewise D_it(E) profiles without
asserting calibration. This review identifies experimental techniques,
source classes, and minimum quality criteria for evidence-informed bins.

## Candidate Experimental/Measurement Techniques

| Technique | What it can inform | Energy resolution | Stack dependence | Transferability to MVP | Risk |
|-----------|--------------------|-------------------|------------------|------------------------|------|
| Charge pumping | Integrated N_it near midgap | Low (~0.1 eV band) | High | E1 shape prior only | Overinterpretation of single-point as profile |
| C-V / G-V conductance | D_it(E) across upper/lower bandgap | Medium (~10 meV bins) | High | E1/E2 shape prior | Technique-specific extraction model |
| DLTS / transient | Deep-level trap density vs. energy | High (individual levels) | Very high | E1 only (different trap population) | Sees bulk traps, not interface traps |
| DCIV | Interface recombination profile | Medium | High | E1/E2 conditional | Requires recombination model |
| Noise spectroscopy (1/f) | Effective TLF density at Fermi level | Low (single energy point) | Moderate | E1 charge-noise context | Cannot resolve D_it(E) shape |
| STM/STS | Local D_it at atomic scale | Very high | Very high | E1 only (different scale) | Not transferable to ensemble average |

### Key Observations

1. **C-V conductance method** provides the most direct D_it(E) profile
   across energy, making it the most relevant technique for informing
   piecewise bins at E1/E2 level.
2. **Charge pumping** gives integrated counts, useful for validating
   total N_it but not individual bin values.
3. **Noise spectroscopy** constrains the effective trap density at the
   Fermi level but cannot resolve the full energy profile.
4. No single technique provides a complete, transferable D_it(E) profile.

## Candidate Source Classes

| Source class | Example use | Evidence level | Suitable for bins? | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|----------------|--------------------|-------|
| Textbook/reference ranges | D_it ~10¹⁰–10¹² | E1 | Limited (flat bins) | Baseline only |
| Literature D_it(E) curve | U-shape from C-V | E1/E2 | Yes (digitized) | Source-specific |
| Same-material stack data | D_it from same oxide | E2/E3 | Yes (cautious) | Requires stack matching |
| Own experimental data | Device measurement | E3/E4 | Future | Not available now |

## Minimum Source Quality Criteria

A source may inform P1 bins only if it provides:

1. **Material/interface identified** — e.g., Si(100)/thermal SiO₂.
2. **Extraction technique identified** — e.g., conductance method at 300K.
3. **Units clear** — D_it in eV⁻¹·cm⁻² or equivalent.
4. **Energy range clear** — E relative to band edge or midgap.
5. **Trap type identified** — interface states, not bulk traps or border traps.
6. **Context for transferability** — oxide thickness, anneal conditions, etc.

Sources failing any criterion may provide E0/E1 scale context only.

## Typical Literature D_it(E) Shapes

Based on C-V conductance studies of Si/SiO₂ (Sze & Ng 2006; Nicollian &
Brews 1982; Fleetwood et al.):

- **U-shaped** distribution: minimum near midgap, rising toward band edges.
- Midgap D_it ≈ 10¹⁰ eV⁻¹·cm⁻² (high-quality passivated).
- Band-edge D_it ≈ 10¹¹–10¹² eV⁻¹·cm⁻² (within ~0.1 eV of edges).
- Shape depends on oxidation conditions, anneal, and crystal orientation.

This U-shape could inform a 3-bin P1 profile:
- Low bin (midgap): D_it ~ 10¹⁰
- Medium bins (transition): D_it ~ 5×10¹⁰
- High bins (near band edge): D_it ~ 10¹¹–10¹²

Such a profile would be classified **E1** (literature-informed shape prior).

## Non-Calibration Statement

> **The sources reviewed here may provide literature-scale or experimental-profile
> priors. They do not calibrate this MVP, do not validate device-level behavior,
> and do not authorize ρ_eff, t_eff, solver coupling, or physical φ
> interpretation.**
